Appealing A Ticket

When you are given a parking ticket, the first reaction is normally panic.
That’s a lot of money that most people just don’t have to fork up.
Depending on the situation, you are most likely able to fight some sort of appeal.


1. Access The Situation

Before determining if you should make an appeal.
First be honest with yourself and answer if this situation was your fault or not.
If you were truly parked illegally, then making an appeal will only waste time in the end.
But if you feel as if you were falsely given a parking ticket, then consider a letter of appeal.


2. Letter of Appeal

A Letter of Appeal is sent to the court about the parking ticket. The letter should be polite and honest.
Do not try to make up parts of the story or skew parts to support your claims.
You want to have the letter contain clear facts which you should hopefully have evidence to support, attached to the letter.

The letter should not insult the officer that gave you the ticket.
It should not make any suggestion that the officer was filling a quota, lying, or anything similar.
Doing this could ultimately make whatever else you say become pointless to the clerk reading your letter of appeal.

For the letter you want to include the following things:
  1. Citation Number
  2. Date & Time Ticket Issued
  3. Location Ticket Issued
  4. Parking Law Allegedly Violated
  5. Evidence Support Your Appeal
